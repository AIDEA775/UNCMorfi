¿Windows? ¿How estimates work?

Estimates are a foolish and innocent approximation of what you might expect in the dining room row. It is not accurate, we know it. The number of windows in front of you in the row is in the slider bar. A window is literally a window, you can also think of it as columns, they are along the dining room on both sides. For example, from the pinwheel (where you pass the card) to the men's restrooms, the length of the row is 6 windows (count them), from one side of the dining room to the other there are 12 windows, the curve (of the main entrance) counts as one more window, and so you keep adding them until you reach the place where you are (up to the side entrance there are 25 windows). This is an early experimental function. We choose the windows as measure by the changing nature of the row, the curves and countercurves may vary from day to day.

I did not understand how the windows are counted

Surely you'll understand if we explain it mathematically: Let "V" be the distance between any pair of consecutive columns, let "D" be the distance you'll have to travel until you reach the pinwheel and let "N" be a natural number: Then you are at N windows if N*V is the best approximation to D.

Why are Internet and camera permissions needed?

  • Internet access is necessary for updating the menu, the balance and the measurer.
  • Camera access is for adding the card by scanning the barcode on it.

Do I need Internet to check my card balance?

All the data is stored on the device to check offline, but you need to be connected to the Internet to get it updated.

Do I can eat twice in one day?

No.

What is the bell that rings every so often in the dining room?

Legend has it that when the bell rings there is rotation among the cooks.
